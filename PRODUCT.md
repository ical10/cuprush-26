# CupRush 26 Product Context

## Product

CupRush 26 is a mobile-first prediction game built for the 2026 international football tournament. Fans answer fast match and stat questions, save their picks through an invisible sponsored Solana flow, follow live outcomes, build streaks, and climb the leaderboard.

The product is entertainment, not betting. It offers no deposits, wagers, tradable positions, or cash rewards.

## Register

**Default: Product surface.** Design serves a repeated task: understand one question, make one pick, see it lock, follow the match, and move to the next card.

Marketing and share tiles may use the brand register, but app screens must preserve clear controls, stable navigation, semantic states, readable data, and restrained motion.

## Audience

- Casual football fans arriving through a shared link or QR code
- Mobile users watching or anticipating a live match
- Fans with no crypto knowledge and no patience for wallet jargon
- International supporters who recognize simple football language more readily than technical terminology

They arrive excited, distracted, and ready to participate. Give them a meaningful choice within seconds.

## Purpose

Turn passive match watching into a quick shared ritual: make the call, lock it in, follow the tension, and prove you saw the moment coming.

## Brand Promise

**Join the rush. Make the call. Feel like a winner before the final whistle.**

CupRush 26 should create pre-victory adrenaline: the confidence and belonging of backing your colors before the result is known.

## Name and Rally Line

- **Name:** CupRush 26
- **Primary rally line:** The match is live. Make your call. Be the winner.
- **Compact CTA line:** Make your call.
- **Share line:** I made the call. Can you beat it?

The name has passed a preliminary web screen only. Complete formal trademark and domain clearance before public launch.

## Positioning

CupRush 26 is the instant prediction layer for the tournament: faster than a fantasy league, more participatory than a scores app, and free of betting mechanics.

Lead with football and fan competition. Solana is proof infrastructure, not the product story.

## Personality

- **Electric, not chaotic** — high energy with a clear next action
- **Triumphant, not arrogant** — celebrate courage and participation without guaranteeing results
- **Fiercely loyal, not hostile** — channel supporter devotion without abuse or tribal exclusion
- **Futuristic, not dystopian** — match-night technology, not cyberpunk grime
- **Playful, not childish** — fast, social, and expressive without mascots or novelty copy taking over

If CupRush 26 were a supporter, it would be the friend who pulls everyone into the chant, calls the next goal with complete conviction, and laughs when the match proves them wrong.

## Voice

Use active, compact sentences. Sound like match-day momentum, not commentary analysis.

- Put the action first: “Pick a side.” “Lock it in.” “Watch it live.”
- Celebrate participation before correctness.
- Use football terms that casual fans understand.
- Treat errors calmly and explain the next move.
- Use “prediction” or “pick,” never “bet,” “stake,” or “winnings.”
- Mention Solana only when confirmation or disclosure requires it.

## Design Principles

1. **One decision owns the screen.** The current question and its outcomes dominate.
2. **Momentum stays visible.** Show match state, streak, and deck progress without turning the screen into a dashboard.
3. **Every state has a signal.** Pair color with words, icons, or shape.
4. **Energy follows events.** Motion responds to picks, live changes, and results; decoration stays still.
5. **Football before technology.** The interface feels like a night match, not a wallet or trading terminal.
6. **Confidence without false certainty.** The fan may feel like a winner; the product never promises one.

## References

- Cyberpunk game interfaces for neon contrast, sharp geometry, and kinetic energy
- Elite football clubs for loyalty, confidence, and match-night ceremony
- Major international football events for scale, anticipation, inclusivity, and global celebration

References set emotional territory, not elements to copy.

## Anti-References

- FIFA trophy imagery, official emblems, official type, official slogans, or a trophy-plus-26 lockup
- Sportsbooks, odds tables, casino lighting, chips, coins, cash, or “win big” language
- Crypto dashboards, wallet-first onboarding, token imagery, purple blockchain gradients
- Generic esports interfaces with dense chrome, tiny labels, and constant glow
- Dystopian grime, weapons, skulls, glitch noise, or hostile supporter imagery
- Flag mosaics or host-country stereotypes as the primary identity

## Accessibility

- Meet WCAG 2.1 AA contrast for text and interactive states.
- Keep every touch target at least 44 by 44 CSS pixels.
- Preserve button alternatives for all swipe actions.
- Never use color alone for winning, losing, live, settled, or failed states.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; keep the full interaction understandable without animation.
- Use plain English, short labels, and one sentence per decision.
- Keep keyboard focus visible and navigation order predictable.

## Product Constraints

- Mobile-first PWA with a practical maximum content width
- Live state arrives through Server-Sent Events and may reconnect or lag
- Team names and translated labels may be long
- Guests can try a card before authentication
- Authentication, wallet creation, and sponsored chain submission must stay secondary to the pick
- Delayed settlement, push, void, offline, and failure states are normal product states

## Success Test

A first-time fan should understand the question, make a pick, and know what happened next without knowing anything about Solana. The experience should feel unmistakably built for the 2026 tournament while remaining visually original and unofficial.
