## Executive Summary

CupRush 26 sounds like the supporter who pulls everyone into the moment. It uses short, active language to create FOMO, confidence, and match-day momentum. The fan should feel that making a call is already an act worth celebrating, even before the result arrives.

The voice stays competitive without becoming hostile, celebratory without promising victory, and futuristic without talking like technology. Football leads. Solana appears only when the product must explain confirmation or wallet behavior.

## We Are / We Are Not

| We Are | We Are Not |
|---|---|
| **Electric** — immediate and full of match-day momentum | **Chaotic** — noisy, breathless, or hard to follow |
| **Triumphant** — fans feel bold for making the call | **Overpromising** — every fan cannot literally win |
| **Fiercely loyal** — shared colors and commitment matter | **Hostile** — rivals remain part of the fun |
| **Inclusive** — casual fans can join in seconds | **Insider-only** — jargon never becomes a gate |
| **Future-facing** — live, responsive, and sharp | **Tech-obsessed** — chains and wallets do not lead |
| **Playful** — quick, social, and spirited | **Childish** — novelty never weakens the competition |

### Voice Attributes

#### Electric

- **What it means:** Create forward motion with compact verbs and immediate stakes.
- **How it shows up:** “Kickoff is close. Make your call.”
- **Avoid:** Repeated exclamation marks, all-caps paragraphs, filler hype.
- **Evidence:** The user wants fans excited, hyped, and afraid to miss the fun.
- **Confidence:** High

#### Triumphant

- **What it means:** Treat participation as confident and memorable before the score settles.
- **How it shows up:** “Your call is locked. Now own the moment.”
- **Avoid:** “Guaranteed winner,” “easy win,” or mocking wrong picks.
- **Evidence:** The user wants fans to “feel like a winner already.”
- **Confidence:** High

#### Fiercely Loyal

- **What it means:** Speak to the pride of backing a side and standing by a call.
- **How it shows up:** “You picked your side. Stay with it.”
- **Avoid:** Aggression, abuse, nationalism, or exclusion.
- **Evidence:** The desired supporter culture is enthusiastic, passionate, and fiercely loyal.
- **Confidence:** High

#### Inclusive

- **What it means:** Make every instruction understandable to a casual fan with no crypto knowledge.
- **How it shows up:** “Save my pick,” then confirm it quietly.
- **Avoid:** PDA, transaction hash, delegation, gas, stake, oracle, or protocol in routine UI.
- **Evidence:** The PRD names casual mobile fans and explicitly removes crypto friction.
- **Confidence:** High

#### Future-Facing

- **What it means:** Sound live and responsive rather than robotic or sci-fi theatrical.
- **How it shows up:** “The match changed. So did your position.”
- **Avoid:** “Initiating protocol,” hacker slang, or dystopian cyberpunk role-play.
- **Evidence:** The user chose cyberpunk energy while the product requires simple fan language.
- **Confidence:** Medium

## Messaging Framework

### Primary Value Proposition

Make fast calls during the 2026 tournament, follow them live, and prove your football instinct.

### Rally Line

**The match is live. Make your call. Be the winner.**

Use the full line on campaign and onboarding surfaces. In product UI, shorten it to the action the fan needs now.

### Key Message Pillars

1. **Join the moment**
   - Core idea: Watching becomes more exciting when the fan commits to a call.
   - Use for: acquisition, shared links, QR entry, empty deck.
   - Example: “The match is moving. Get your call in.”

2. **Own your instinct**
   - Core idea: A clear prediction turns opinion into friendly competition.
   - Use for: card deck, save prompt, locked state.
   - Example: “You saw it coming. Lock it in.”

3. **Follow every swing**
   - Core idea: Live match events change the fan’s position in real time.
   - Use for: live screen, reconnect, settling.
   - Example: “One corner changed the call. You’re ahead.”

4. **Climb together**
   - Core idea: Streaks and leaderboards make the tournament social without cash rewards.
   - Use for: results, leaderboard, sharing.
   - Example: “Three right in a row. Who catches you now?”

### Positioning Boundaries

- Versus fantasy leagues: instant decisions, no squad management.
- Versus score apps: participation, not passive updates.
- Versus sportsbooks: points and pride only; no money or odds.
- Versus crypto apps: football first; chain confirmation stays behind the experience.

## Terminology Guide

### Must-Use Terms

| Term | Usage | Instead of | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Call | The fan’s prediction as an act of confidence | Bet, wager | “Make your call.” |
| Pick | The selected outcome | Position, order | “Your pick is locked.” |
| Points | Leaderboard scoring | Winnings, payout | “You earned 1 point.” |
| Streak | Consecutive correct calls | Run rate | “Your streak is now 4.” |
| Live | A match or state updating now | Real-time feed | “Follow it live.” |

### Preferred Terms

| Term | Usage | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Lock | Confirmation before the deadline | “Lock my pick.” |
| Ahead / Behind | Current state with supporting stat | “Ahead: 5 corners to 3.” |
| Settling | CupRush verifies the result | “Settling the final result.” |
| Push | Equal result with no streak loss | “Push. Your streak stays intact.” |
| Void | The question cannot settle | “Void. This call does not affect your score.” |

### Avoid These Terms

| Term | Reason | Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| Submit | Generic and bureaucratic | Lock my pick |
| Transaction | Exposes implementation too early | Confirmation |
| Wallet | Use only in disclosure or profile | Account |
| Guaranteed | Makes a false promise | Confident, bold |
| Defeat / loser | Shames participation | Not this time |

### Never-Use Terms

| Term | Reason |
|---|---|
| Bet, wager, stake | Misrepresents the no-money game |
| Odds, payout, jackpot | Creates sportsbook or casino meaning |
| Guaranteed winner | False and unsafe promise |
| Official World Cup game | Implies affiliation or endorsement |

## Language Patterns

### Patterns That Work

1. **Action + immediate consequence:** “Lock your pick. Start your streak.”
2. **Short tension turn:** “Ahead—for now.”
3. **Participation as status:** “Your call is in.”
4. **Friendly challenge:** “Can your friends beat that?”
5. **Calm recovery:** “Connection dropped. Catching up now.”

### Anti-Patterns

1. **Empty hype:** “Experience the ultimate revolutionary fan engagement platform.”
   - Better: “Make the call. Follow it live.”
2. **Crypto-first copy:** “Sign this delegated Solana transaction.”
   - Better: “Approve once so CupRush can save your picks.”
3. **Casino energy:** “Win big on every match.”
   - Better: “Build your streak across the tournament.”
4. **Shaming:** “Wrong! You lost.”
   - Better: “Not this time. Next call?”

>

>

Why it works: The rally line creates energy, the second sentence explains the loop, and the CTA starts the task.

### Guest Save Prompt

> **Your call deserves a place on the board.**
>
> Save your pick and start your streak.
>
> **Save my pick**

Why it works: It respects the choice already made and postpones account mechanics until needed.

### Confirmation

> **Your call is locked.**
>
> We saved it on Solana. No fees, no wallet setup.

Why it works: Confidence leads; infrastructure appears as brief reassurance.

### Correct Result

> **You called it.**
>
> Four right in a row. The next challenge is live.
>
> **Keep the streak alive**

### Incorrect Result

> **Not this time.**
>
> One call ended. Another is waiting.
>
> **Make the next call**

### Delayed Settlement

> **Final whistle. Result still checking.**
>
> Your pick is safe. We’ll update it as soon as we verify the match data.

Why it works: It names the delay, preserves trust, and avoids unsupported timing promises.
